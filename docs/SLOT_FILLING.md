# Joint Intent Detection + BIO Slot Filling (offline only)

`SLOT_SCHEMA_VERSION = "1.0.0"`

Three ideas were ported from an academic SLU benchmark repo
(`slot_filling_and_intent_detection_of_SLU`) onto Sevi's own stack: a BIO slot-annotation
corpus format, a joint intent + slot model architecture, and span-level evaluation
conventions.

**This is offline research tooling. It is not wired into the running chatbot.** A flag-gated
shadow path through `AdvancedNLUEngine.enhance_prediction()` was built and measured, then
deliberately **not merged** — see section 8. `api/nlu_engine.py` is untouched, so live
behaviour is unchanged by construction rather than by a runtime flag.

---

## 1. What this is and what it is not

**Every builder must print the unflattering number next to the flattering one. A report that
omits `n_gold_spans`, `all_O_gold_fraction`, or the circularity caveat is non-conforming and
must be rejected.**

### What this WILL deliver

- A real, inspectable ATIS-format BIO corpus at `data/slots/`, byte-compatible with the
  reference repo's format.
- A genuine joint intent+slot BiLSTM in the project's own stack, trainable and loadable.
- A real conlleval span-F1 implementation with hand-verified edge-case tests -- this is the one
  component with no caveats attached, because arithmetic on given inputs is exactly what it
  claims to be.
- A reusable offline pipeline: build the corpus, train, and score spans, with no production
  wiring and therefore no production risk.
- Honest documentation of all of the above.

### What this will NOT deliver

- **It will not improve answer quality.** Nothing here changes routing, response selection, or the
  intent decision. `api/nlu_engine.py` and `api/hybrid_chatbot.py` are both untouched, so live
  behaviour is identical to before this feature existed.
- **It will not produce a defensible slot-filling accuracy claim.** The training labels are
  generated by closed-list gazetteers. A model trained on them has no signal beyond them, so
  scoring it against the same weak labels measures memorisation, not capability. **A span-F1 of
  0.90 here would mean the model learned the gazetteer -- not that it can fill slots.** No number
  from `models/eval_joint.json` may be quoted externally without the circularity caveat attached.
- **It will not beat `api/campus_context.py`.** `extract_campus()` already does `campus_name` in
  production today with a maintained alias table. One of our five types is a re-implementation of
  working code.
- **It will not demonstrate bilingual slot coverage.** 435 patterns are Tagalog or code-switched,
  but the slot fillers inside them are almost always English loan terms. There is no
  Tagalog-specific slot vocabulary in this corpus to learn.

### The realistic ceiling, stated in numbers

- 3135 patterns; median length **3 tokens**; 56.6% are <= 3 tokens; 113 are a single token. The
  reference ATIS train file is ~4478 utterances at ~11 tokens with dense multi-slot annotation.
  **We are porting a format designed for sentences onto a corpus of keyword stubs.**
- Under the earlier eight-type inventory, 29.8% of lines carried a span. The shipped five-type
  inventory is a strict subset with two additional blocklists and two suppression rules, so
  **expect roughly 20-22% slot-bearing, i.e. ~78-80% all-O.** The exact figure is whatever
  `data/slots/build_report.json` says -- quote that, not this estimate.
- The 10% test split is ~314 lines, of which perhaps **~65 carry any span, giving on the order of
  70-80 gold spans total.** At that sample size a single span is worth ~1.3 F1 points and the 95%
  interval on span F1 is roughly **+/-10 points**. Any comparison of two configurations that
  differ by less than 10 points is noise, and must be reported as noise.
- Two of the five types are near-degenerate: `grade_value` is dominated by one mechanical template
  family (`what does 1.0 mean` x11) -- which is why the split is group-aware, and why its F1 will
  look worse than a random split would have shown, correctly. `campus_name` spans are frequently
  the entire utterance (`CvSU Naic`), making span tagging and intent classification the same
  decision.
- Only ~4% of lines carry two or more slot types. **There is almost nothing in this corpus for a
  sequence model to learn about slot interaction**, which is the entire reason joint models exist.
  That is the honest answer to "did the joint architecture earn its keep": on this corpus, no --
  the architecture is justified by what it would allow later, not by what it demonstrates now.

### Measured, as built

From `data/slots/build_report.json` (seed 42). Quote these, not the estimates above.

| | predicted above | **actually built** |
|---|---|---|
| rows out | 3135 | **3135** (0 skipped, 0 deduped) |
| all-O fraction | 78-80% | **80.7%** (605 of 3135 rows carry any span) |
| spans total | -- | **622** (`campus_place` 281, `document_type` 110, `campus_name` 102, `grade_value` 72, `program_name` 57) |
| split | 80/10/10 | **2507 / 314 / 314** over 2993 groups |
| gold spans in test | ~70-80 | **66** |
| rows with 2+ slot types | ~4% | **6 rows, 0.19%** |
| tokens over `MAX_LEN` | <1% | **0** (max 16 tokens, median 3) |

Three of those are worse than forecast and must not be smoothed over:

1. **Multi-slot rows are 0.19%, not 4%** -- twenty times rarer than estimated. Six rows in the
   entire corpus contain two slot types. Whatever the joint BiLSTM learns, it will not be slot
   interaction; there is effectively no such signal present.
2. **80.7% all-O**, above the top of the forecast band. The `--o-weight 0.20` down-weight is
   carrying more load than planned.
3. **The who-is and dean's-list suppressions fired 0 times each** (`suppressed.who_is = 0`,
   `suppressed.deans_list = 0`). Those two rules were the stated justification for cutting the
   `person_role` slot type. They cost nothing, but they also bought nothing measurable on this
   corpus -- the cut stands on the overlap argument alone. Only 4 blocklist hits occurred.

`MAX_LEN = 24` is confirmed as generous: the longest tokenized pattern is 16 tokens.

## 2. The three ported ideas

Source: `slot_filling_and_intent_detection_of_SLU` (academic SLU benchmark repo).

| # | Idea | Where it came from | Where it lives here |
|---|---|---|---|
| i | **BIO slot-annotation corpus format** -- `token:TAG ... <=> intent`, one utterance per line, with sibling `vocab.slot` / `vocab.intent` | `data/atis-2/{train,valid,test}` in the reference repo | `data/slots/*`, written by `scripts/build_slot_corpus.py` |
| ii | **Joint intent detection + slot filling** -- one shared BiLSTM encoder, a per-token slot softmax and a pooled intent softmax, trained together (Liu & Lane, 2016, "Attention-Based Recurrent Network Models for Joint Intent Detection and Slot Filling") | the repo's joint model family | `training/train_joint_nlu.py`, `api/joint_nlu.py` |
| iii | **Evaluation conventions** -- conlleval exact-span P/R/F1, semantic frame accuracy, and the ATIS `#` multi-intent scoring trick | the repo's scoring scripts | `api/slot_metrics.py`, `scripts/eval_joint_nlu.py` |

### No code was taken, and why

The reference repo targets Python 3.6, `torch==1.4`, `allennlp`, and `transformers==2.5`, and its
code directories are deleted in its own working tree. Sevi runs no PyTorch and no
`transformers` at all -- the stack is TensorFlow/Keras, scikit-learn, numpy, FastAPI. Adding
dependencies was out of scope. Every line here is a reimplementation on Sevi's existing stack,
following the reference repo's *format and conventions* so the corpus stays byte-compatible and
the numbers stay comparable to published SLU results.

Attention is **not** ported. Liu & Lane's attention variant needs sentence-length inputs to pay
off; the median utterance here is three tokens. A plain BiLSTM is the honest choice at this size.

## 3. Slot inventory

Five slot types, **11 tags** (`B-` and `I-` for each, plus `O`).

| Type | Meaning | Weak-label source |
|---|---|---|
| `campus_name` | which CvSU campus the query concerns | `api/campus_context.py` `CAMPUSES` aliases (+ a `Dasmarinas`/`Dasmarinas` entry added in the builder's own gazetteer table only) |
| `campus_place` | any named physical location or service point: buildings, colleges, amenities, offices | `api/campus_places.py`, `api/campus_directory.py`, `api/campus_context.py` place literals, `data/waypoints_override.json`, `data/custom_markers.json` |
| `document_type` | a named record, credential or certificate a student requests, submits, replaces or authenticates | hand-written closed list in `scripts/build_slot_corpus.py` |
| `program_name` | a degree program or field of study | closed list harvested once from the eight program intents, plus the acronym set |
| `grade_value` | a CvSU grade point, grade category or grade-average term | pure rule: `^[1-5]\.(?:0\|00\|25\|5\|50\|75)$`, `{INC, DRP, GWA}`, `incomplete/passing/failing grade` |

Boundary rules that are easy to get wrong:

- `campus_name` **excludes** a trailing `campus`. `CvSU Imus` -> span `Imus`; `Cavite City Campus`
  -> span `Cavite City`.
- `grade_value` is the number/term **only**. `grade of 5.0` -> span `5.0`.

### What was deliberately cut, and why

| Cut | Reason |
|---|---|
| `person_role` | 133 of 173 hits come from three intents -- one templated question family, not a span distribution. It also owned the largest annotation conflict in the corpus (49-pattern overlap with `campus_place`). Replaced by the deterministic who-is suppression rule below. |
| `student_status` | "alumni" alone is 36 of 136 and is usually a topic head, not a status; "shifting" is verbal in most of its 17. Cannot be weak-labeled cleanly. |
| `law_or_policy` | 37 of 70 hits are the token `NSTP` inside one intent. NSTP/CWTS/ROTC are simultaneously law, program and subject -- unresolvable at this corpus size. |
| `org_cvsu` | **Never to be re-added.** It makes the numbers look better while measuring nothing: "CvSU" appears in a large fraction of patterns and tagging it is free recall. |
| `time_period`, `exam_name`, `fee_amount`, `event_name`, `org_external`, `subject_name`, Tagalog interrogative cues | below the learnable floor, or cues rather than spans |

### Disambiguation rules (binding, applied in this order)

1. **`AMBIGUOUS_BLOCKLIST`** -- removed from *all* gazetteers as standalone entries, never produce
   a span alone: `coe`, `cav`, `con`, `cas`, `ced`, `bsa`, `let`, `als`, `may`, `ba`, `po`. They
   remain matchable *inside* longer entries (`College of Engineering`, `Certificate of
   Employment`).
2. **`BARE_HEAD_BLOCKLIST`** -- generic heads excluded as standalone entries: `certificate`,
   `clearance`, `id`, `subject`, `faculty`, `staff`, `dean`, `engineering`, `education`, `office`,
   `program`, `course`, `degree`. Matchable only inside multi-token entries.
3. **Who-is suppression** (this is what replaced `person_role`): if any of `{who, whos, sino}`
   appears among the **first three folded tokens**, and a candidate span's **first token** is in
   `ROLE_HEADS = {dean, deans, registrar, administrator, president, chancellor, counselor,
   director, coordinator, head}`, the span is suppressed. `who is the registrar` -> all-O.
   `go to the registrar` -> `registrar` = `campus_place`.
4. **`dean's list` guard** -- a candidate span starting with `dean` immediately followed by `list`
   or `lister` is suppressed.
5. **Priority on overlap** (higher wins): `grade_value` > `document_type` > `program_name` >
   `campus_name` > `campus_place`.
6. **Longest match wins** within a type, then across types by that priority. Matching is
   left-to-right, non-overlapping, greedy on length.

## 4. File format

```
data/slots/train
data/slots/valid
data/slots/test
data/slots/vocab.slot
data/slots/vocab.intent
data/slots/build_report.json     (builder diagnostics, not part of the ATIS format)
```

These paths are committed and **do** ship in the Docker image. They are deliberately not under
`docs/` or `training/` -- `.dockerignore` excludes both, and that exact mistake once silently
killed the site RAG tier in production.

- Encoding **UTF-8, no BOM**; line ending **`\n` only**, including on the last line. No blank
  lines, no trailing whitespace, no comments, no header.
- One utterance per line: `<tok>:<TAG> <tok>:<TAG> ... <=> <intent>`, separator exactly
  `" <=> "`.
- Token surfaces are written **verbatim, original case preserved**. This diverges from ATIS,
  which is all-lowercase, so the file stays losslessly diff-able against
  `data/cavsu_intents.json`. Lowercasing happens only at vocab-lookup time, via `vocab_key()`.
- `len(tokens) == len(tags)` on every line, always >= 1.
- `<=> <NULL>` is the no-intent form. Our corpus always has an intent, so it never appears; the
  constant exists for format compatibility only.

### `vocab.slot` and `vocab.intent`

`vocab.slot` is a constant: exactly these 11 lines, ASCII-ascending, `O` last.

```
B-campus_name
B-campus_place
B-document_type
B-grade_value
B-program_name
I-campus_name
I-campus_place
I-document_type
I-grade_value
I-program_name
O
```

`vocab.intent` is the deduplicated, ASCII-sorted union of intents across `train + valid + test`
(so an intent that lands only in `valid` still has a stable id). Expected: 127 lines.

### The colon edge case

A token can never contain whitespace, but it **can** be the single character `:`, and a tag never
contains `:`. Serialization is `f"{token}:{tag}"` with no escaping; parsing splits on the **last**
colon.

| text | field | `rsplit(":", 1)` |
|---|---|---|
| `library` | `library:B-campus_place` | `("library", "B-campus_place")` |
| `:` | `::O` | `(":", "O")` |
| any token with N internal colons | `a:b:c:O` | `("a:b:c", "O")` |

`decode_line()` raises `SlotFormatError` if a parsed token is empty, if the tag is not in `TAGS`,
or if `:` is absent.

### Example lines

```
Ladislao:O Diwa:O library:B-campus_place <=> library
CvSU:O Imus:B-campus_name <=> campus_specific
how:O to:O get:O TOR:B-document_type <=> transcript_request_details
what:O does:O 1.25:B-grade_value mean:O <=> retention_policy_grades
BS:B-program_name Midwifery:I-program_name <=> nursing_health_programs
who:O is:O the:O campus:O administrator:O <=> campus_officials
```

Every line above is copied verbatim from the built corpus; none is illustrative.

The first line shows the gazetteer's reach honestly: `Ladislao Diwa` is not an entry,
so only the generic head `library` is tagged and the proper noun is lost. The last line
is all-O because `campus administrator` is not an entry either — **not** because rule 3
fired. The who-is suppression and dean's-list guards match 0 of the 3135 real patterns
(`suppressed` in `build_report.json`); they are dead code against today's data, kept
only because `registrar` would otherwise be mislabeled if a who-is pattern is added later.

### Split

80/10/10, `SPLIT_SEED = 42`, **group-aware and per-intent**. Random line splitting is forbidden:
near-duplicate template families (`what does 1.0 mean` ... x11) would leak across splits and
inflate F1. Lines are grouped by intent plus a placeholder-normalized token skeleton (span heads
become `<slot_type>`, digits become `#`); whole groups move together, so the splits are
group-disjoint by construction. Row order within a file is ascending
`(intent, original_pattern_index)`, so re-running the builder is byte-identical.

## 5. The tokenization divergence (alignment-critical)

This is the single most important implementation detail. It is stated verbatim in
`api/slot_schema.py` immediately above `joint_tokenize`:

```python
# ALIGNMENT-CRITICAL. This is deliberately NOT NeuralNetworkTrainer._preprocess
# (api/hybrid_chatbot.py:1902). That function lowercases -> deletes every char
# outside [a-z0-9\s] -> nltk.word_tokenize -> WordNet-lemmatizes -> rejoins.
# Measured effects: "Kumusta CvSU?" 3->2 tokens (? deleted); "paano mag-OJT" ->
# "paano magojt" (token rewritten); "students" -> "student"; every non-ASCII
# character (n-tilde, peso sign) deleted. That destroys the label[i] <-> token[i]
# invariant that slot filling requires. It is fine for whole-sentence intent
# classification, which is why production still uses it. The joint path receives
# the RAW text at api/nlu_engine.py:225 and must keep it raw.
```

Re-measured against the live `_preprocess` (`api/hybrid_chatbot.py:1902`, `_NON_ALPHA_RE =
r"[^a-z0-9\s]"`) and against `joint_tokenize`:

| input | `_preprocess` output | `joint_tokenize` output | damage |
|---|---|---|---|
| `Kumusta CvSU?` | `kumusta cvsu` (2 tok) | `['Kumusta', 'CvSU', '?']` (3 tok) | token deleted |
| `paano mag-OJT` | `paano magojt` (2 tok) | `['paano', 'mag-OJT']` (2 tok) | token rewritten |
| `requirements for students` | `requirement for student` | `['requirements', 'for', 'students']` | two tokens rewritten |
| `what does 1.25 mean` | `what doe 125 mean` | `['what', 'does', '1.25', 'mean']` | **the grade value is destroyed**; `does` -> `doe` |
| `magkano ang tuition? ₱1,500.00` | `magkano ang tuition 150000` (4 tok) | `['magkano','ang','tuition','?','₱','1,500.00']` (6 tok) | currency mangled, 2 tokens deleted |
| `Dasmariñas Campus` | `dasmarias campus` | `['Dasmariñas', 'Campus']` | diacritic character deleted |
| `dean's office` | `dean office` | `["dean's", 'office']` | apostrophe token merged |

Any of these breaks `label[i] <-> token[i]`. `what does 1.25 mean` is the clearest case: the only
slot in the utterance ceases to exist before the model ever sees it.

`joint_tokenize` is **lossless**: every non-whitespace character is consumed by exactly one token,
so `text[start:end]` is the verbatim surface of any predicted span. The invariant
`"".join(text[s:e] for _t, s, e in joint_tokenize(text)) == "".join(text.split())` is asserted for
every pattern at corpus-build time.

Normalization happens at exactly two later points, never on the surfaces:
`vocab_key(token) = token.lower()` for embedding lookup, and `fold(token)` (case + diacritic fold)
for gazetteer matching only. Gazetteer matching is **token-level n-gram equality**, never `re`
against raw text -- that removes character-offset mapping, which is where `Don Severino delas
Alas`, `dean's office`, `CvSU-CCAT`, `K-12` and `Dasmariñas` would otherwise have broken.

There is exactly one tokenizer, in `api/slot_schema.py`, imported by the builder, the trainer,
the inference module and the eval script. Production's `_preprocess` is untouched and still owns
the intent classifier.

## 6. Architecture and hyperparameters

Liu & Lane (2016) joint model, one shared encoder and two heads:

```python
inp    = Input(shape=(MAX_LEN,), dtype="int32", name="tokens")            # (None, 24)
emb    = Embedding(n_tokens, EMBEDDING_DIM, mask_zero=True, name="emb")(inp)
d1     = Dropout(0.3, name="emb_drop")(emb)
enc    = Bidirectional(LSTM(LSTM_UNITS, return_sequences=True), name="bilstm")(d1)   # (None, 24, 128)
d2     = Dropout(0.3, name="enc_drop")(enc)
slots  = Dense(n_tags, activation="softmax", name="slots")(d2)            # (None, 24, 11)
pooled = GlobalAveragePooling1D(name="pool")(d2)                          # (None, 128), mask-aware
h      = Dense(64, activation="relu", name="intent_hidden")(pooled)
h      = Dropout(0.3, name="intent_drop")(h)
intent = Dense(n_intents, activation="softmax", name="intent")(h)         # (None, 127)
model  = Model(inp, {"slots": slots, "intent": intent}, name="joint_nlu")
```

Constraints that are not stylistic:

- **Outputs are a dict, never a list.** With list outputs Keras returns them in the order
  `[intent, slots]` -- the opposite of the declaration order. A dict makes blind indexing
  impossible.
- **`input_length=` is forbidden** (deprecated in Keras 3, emits a `UserWarning`).
- **`TimeDistributed` is forbidden** -- a bare `Dense` on a `(None, 24, 128)` tensor already
  yields `(None, 24, 11)`. One less wrapper to serialize.
- **`.h5` is banned for this model; `models/joint_nlu.keras` only.** With `mask_zero=True`,
  `load_model("x.h5")` fails with `ValueError: Unknown layer: 'NotEqual'`. The `.keras` v3 format
  round-trips it, and the mask is worth keeping: `GlobalAveragePooling1D` then divides by the real
  token count instead of by 24, so the intent head is not diluted by padding on the 56% of
  utterances that are <= 3 tokens.
- The target is **Keras 3.14** (what the running stack actually installs, per
  `deployment/requirements_local.txt`), using only constructs that also work unchanged on Keras
  2.14.

| Param | Value | Why |
|---|---|---|
| `MAX_LEN` | 24 | Longest corpus pattern is 14 word-tokens; our tokenizer also emits punctuation, so 24 covers essentially the whole corpus with headroom. Production's 20 is not reused -- it was tuned for a lemmatized, punctuation-stripped stream. |
| `VOCAB_SIZE` | 4000 | Production's 1000 would push most gazetteer surfaces (`Ladislao`, `Maragondon`, `apostille`, `BSHM`) to OOV -- fatal for slot filling, harmless for intent classification. |
| `EMBEDDING_DIM` | 64 | Matches the production NN so the two are comparable. 4000x64 = 256k params is large relative to the data; countered by dropout and early stopping, and reported honestly. |
| `LSTM_UNITS` | 64 (-> 128 after concat) | **Halved from production's 128.** We additionally fit 24x11 per-token softmaxes on ~700 slot-bearing lines; 128 per direction would be pure overfitting capacity. |
| `Dropout` | 0.3 x3 | `recurrent_dropout=0.0` deliberately, to keep the fast fused LSTM kernel. |
| `--o-weight` | 0.20 | The sole mitigation for the ~78% all-O imbalance. Chosen over "train only on slot-bearing lines" (destroys the 127-way intent head) and over "synthesize from templates" (which would make the model a literal reimplementation of the gazetteer). Exposed as a flag so the trade-off can be swept. |
| `batch_size` / `epochs` / `patience` | 32 / 40 / 6 | ~2500 training rows -> ~78 steps/epoch. Early stopping on `val_loss` does the real work; 40 is a ceiling, not a target. |

Sample weights: `0.0` at pad positions (redundant with the mask, kept as visible defence),
`--o-weight` at real `O` tokens, `1.0` at real non-`O` tokens.

Artifacts, all JSON except the model itself -- **no pickles**, which removes the
pickle-deserialization surface `verify_artifact` exists to guard and is immune to
sklearn/keras version drift:

| File | Content |
|---|---|
| `models/joint_nlu.keras` | the model, `.keras` v3 format |
| `models/joint_token_vocab.json` | `{"<pad>": 0, "<unk>": 1, ...}`, keys in ascending id order |
| `models/joint_labels.json` | `{"schema_version", "tags", "intents"}` |
| `models/joint_meta.json` | hyperparameters, counts, and the valid-split metrics |

`models/trusted_hashes.json` is **not** updated and `scripts/update_trusted_hashes.py` is **not**
run. Unknown artifacts only WARN at startup (`api/hybrid_chatbot.py:54-56`), and that script
rewrites the whole manifest from a hardcoded list -- running it would be an unrequested change to
production's verification state. Pinning is a separate, human-approved step after phase 1.

## 7. Metrics

`api/slot_metrics.py` is stdlib-only and imports nothing from this repo, so it can be used and
tested with zero setup.

### Chunk decoding (conlleval / IOB2)

Walk the tags with at most one open chunk:

- `O` or an unrecognized tag: close any open chunk.
- `B-T`: close any open chunk; open `(T, i)`.
- `I-T` with an open chunk of type `T`: extend.
- `I-T` with **no** open chunk: open `(T, i)` (lenient start).
- `I-T` with an open chunk of type `U != T`: close; open `(T, i)`.
- End of sequence: close any open chunk.

Worked example on a deliberately malformed sequence:

```
tags   =  I-a   I-a   I-b   O    B-b   I-b   B-b   O
index     0     1     2     3    4     5     6     7
```

- `i=0` `I-a`, nothing open -> open `(a,0)` (lenient start)
- `i=1` `I-a`, open `a` -> extend
- `i=2` `I-b`, open `a` != `b` -> close `(a,0,2)`; open `(b,2)`
- `i=3` `O` -> close `(b,2,3)`
- `i=4` `B-b` -> open `(b,4)`
- `i=5` `I-b`, open `b` -> extend
- `i=6` `B-b` -> close `(b,4,6)`; open `(b,6)`
- `i=7` `O` -> close `(b,6,7)`

Result: `[("a",0,2), ("b",2,3), ("b",4,6), ("b",6,7)]`.

### P / R / F1

A predicted span is a true positive iff an identical `(type, start, end)` triple exists in gold
**for the same utterance**. No partial credit, no type-only credit, no overlap credit. Micro sums
`tp/fp/fn` across all utterances; macro is the unweighted mean of per-type F1 over types with
`support > 0 or predicted > 0`. Every denominator of 0 yields `0.0`.

### ATIS multi-intent convention

```python
def intent_correct(gold, pred):
    return pred == gold or pred in gold.split("#")
```

ATIS gold intents are occasionally compound (`atis_flight#atis_airfare`) and a single-label
prediction counts as correct when it names any member. Our corpus is single-intent today, so this
reduces to equality; it is implemented for format compatibility and because
`data/eval/mirror_qa.json` already contains five `multi_intent` trap items that would naturally be
annotated this way.

### Semantic frame accuracy

An utterance is a frame hit iff `intent_correct(gold, pred)` **and**
`set(bio_spans(gold)) == set(bio_spans(pred))`. This is the Liu & Lane joint headline and is
always the lowest number reported.

An all-O utterance with the correct intent **is** a frame hit -- both span sets are empty. Because
~78% of utterances are all-O, **`frame_accuracy` on this corpus is largely a restatement of intent
accuracy** and must not be read as a strong slot result.

### Diagnostics that must always be reported alongside

`n_gold_spans`, `n_pred_spans`, `all_O_gold_fraction`, `tag_accuracy` (labelled inflated), and
per-label `support`. A report missing any of these is non-conforming. `tag_accuracy` is
token-level exact tag match; an all-O predictor scores span-F1 = 0.0 while scoring tag accuracy
above 0.7 on this corpus, which is precisely why span F1 is the headline. That degenerate case is
pinned by case 26 of `test_slot_metrics.py`.

## 8. How to run

In order. Each step fails cleanly with `[ERROR] ...` and exit 1 if its upstream artifact is
missing.

```bash
# 1. build the weakly-labeled BIO corpus  ->  data/slots/*
python scripts/build_slot_corpus.py

# 2. train the joint model  ->  models/joint_*
python training/train_joint_nlu.py

# 3. score the joint model on the held-out split  ->  models/eval_joint.json
python scripts/eval_joint_nlu.py

# 4. score the EXISTING regex EntityExtractor on the same split, and print the
#    joint-vs-regex delta  ->  models/eval_joint_baseline.json
python scripts/eval_joint_nlu.py --baseline
```

Step 4 is not optional. Without it there is no way to say whether the model beats the regexes it
would replace. Run step 3 first, then step 4 -- the baseline run reads the joint report and prints
the side-by-side table with the noise caveat attached.

Useful flags: `--limit N`, `--min-f1 X` (exit 1 below the gate; default `0.0`, i.e. no gate),
`--gold PATH`, `--quiet`. `--baseline` automatically redirects `--out`, `--preds` and `--failures`
to `*_baseline.json` so a baseline run can never clobber the joint report.

### The regex baseline, precisely

`--baseline` projects `EntityExtractor.ENTITY_PATTERNS` onto the slot inventory:

| regex entity type | scored as |
|---|---|
| `document` | `document_type` |
| `program` | `program_name` |
| `facility` | `campus_place` |
| `date`, `fee`, `time`, `contact` | dropped -- no slot counterpart |

Two things must be said out loud when quoting that comparison:

1. The regex extractor has **no `campus_name` and no `grade_value` pattern at all**, so its recall
   on those two types is 0 by construction, not by failure. `api/campus_context.py`
   `extract_campus()` -- which is *not* part of `EntityExtractor` -- already does `campus_name` in
   production and would score far better. The baseline is a floor for `EntityExtractor`
   specifically, not for "everything Sevi can already do".
2. The comparison is **biased in the joint model's favour**: the model was trained on labels
   produced by the same gazetteers it is scored against; the regexes were not. A joint win here is
   necessary evidence, not sufficient evidence.

Read this the unflattering way first:

- **66 gold spans.** The 95% interval on span F1 at this sample size is roughly +/-10 points, so

  span f1 of 0.689 means it recovered roughly two thirds of its own labeler, not that it fills
  slots well.
- **Intent accuracy is 47.1%** on a 127-way problem with a group-aware split -- far below the
  production NB/NN cascade, and the reason the joint intent is shadow-only and never routes.
- **Frame accuracy 43.6% is mostly a restatement of intent accuracy**, because 79.6% of test
  utterances carry no span at all and an all-O utterance with the right intent counts as a frame
  hit.

Nothing here is a slot-filling quality claim. See section 9.

### Why there is no production integration

A shadow path through `AdvancedNLUEngine.enhance_prediction()` was built and measured, then
reverted. `api/nlu_engine.py` is unmodified. The measurements that killed it:

| | median | p95 |
|---|---|---|
| without the joint path | 0.025 ms | -- |
| with the joint path | 1.716 ms | 2.603 ms |
| **added per turn** | **1.691 ms** | -- |

1.7 ms is comfortably inside any latency budget, so cost was not the objection. The objection is
that it **buys nothing**: nothing routes on the output, the joint intent head scores 47.1% against
the production cascade's live 51.1%, and the cost is paid on *every* turn because `_nb_result`
calls `enhance_prediction` unconditionally. Paying a real cost on every request for a value of
zero is not a trade worth making, and a dormant flag in the hot path is a maintenance liability
that outlives whoever remembers why it is there.

If a future phase justifies wiring it, the shape that was validated is: read the flag once in
`__init__`; lazy-import so TensorFlow never loads at module import; attempt the load once per
process under a lock and cache a negative result; validate the returned dict's keys before
merging so a partial result cannot `KeyError` into the chat path; add keys only, never rewrite
`intent`/`confidence`/`entities`. Every failure mode -- missing artifacts, TensorFlow
`ImportError`/`RuntimeError`/`OSError`, tag/token length mismatch, malformed BIO -- must degrade
silently to the existing regex behaviour.

## 9. Circularity and the human-annotation gap

The gold labels in `data/slots/` are produced by `scripts/build_slot_corpus.py` from closed-list
gazetteers. A model trained on them cannot contain information the gazetteers did not, so scoring
it against those same labels measures how well it memorised the gazetteer. That is a real result
-- it tells us the architecture, the tokenizer and the pipeline work -- but it is not a
slot-filling accuracy claim.

Closing the gap requires a human-annotated test set that the labeler never touched:

1. Sample **150+ real utterances** from the Postgres chat history, stratified so slot-bearing
   queries are not swamped by greetings. Do this in a separate, reviewed step -- **chat history
   must not be pulled into `data/slots/` in this phase**, and any sample must be PII-scrubbed
   before it leaves the database.
2. Have them annotated by a person who **has not read the gazetteers**, working only from the slot
   definitions and boundary rules in section 3 of this document.
3. Write the result to `data/slots/human_test` in exactly the format of section 4.
4. Score with `python scripts/eval_joint_nlu.py --gold data/slots/human_test`, and score the regex
   baseline on the same file.
5. Measure inter-annotator agreement on a 30-utterance overlap. If two humans cannot agree on the
   boundaries, the type definition is the problem, not the model.

**No external claim about slot-filling quality may be made until `data/slots/human_test` exists.**
Until then, `models/eval_joint.json` is an engineering smoke test, and its `caveats` array says so
in five places.

## 10. Known limitations

1. **Tokens beyond `MAX_LEN` (24) are never tagged.** They are truncated away before the model
   sees them and are emitted as `O` so that `len(bio) == len(tokens)` always holds. A slot in
   token 25 of a long question is invisible.
2. **Offline only.** Nothing routes on the joint output because nothing reads it at runtime; the
   model is loaded solely by `scripts/eval_joint_nlu.py`.
3. **Weak supervision ceiling.** The corpus is machine-labeled. Recall is capped by the gazetteers;
   precision is capped by the blocklists and the two suppression rules. Anything the gazetteer
   misses is silently labeled `O`, which trains the model to miss it too.
4. **80.7% of lines are all-O**, and only **0.19% (6 rows)** carry two or more slot types -- the
   measured figure from `build_report.json`, twenty times rarer than the ~4% forecast. There is
   effectively no slot-interaction signal here for a sequence model to learn.
5. **The test split is small.** ~70-80 gold spans means +/-10 F1 points of noise. Two
   configurations differing by less than that are indistinguishable.
6. **Template-family concentration.** `grade_value` is dominated by one mechanical family. The
   group-aware split deliberately makes its score look worse than a random split would -- that is
   the honest number, not a regression.
7. **One of the five types is a re-implementation.** `api/campus_context.py extract_campus()`
   already does `campus_name` in production, with a hand-maintained alias table that is easier to
   fix than a retrained model.
8. **No Tagalog slot vocabulary.** Code-switched patterns exist, but their slot fillers are
   English loan terms. Bilingual slot coverage is not demonstrated by this corpus.
9. **No CI wiring.** `.github/workflows/ci.yml` runs two root tests and was not extended in this
   phase. `test_slot_metrics.py` and `test_joint_nlu_contract.py` must be run manually.
10. **Artifacts are unpinned.** `models/joint_*` are not in `models/trusted_hashes.json`, so they
    load with a `[WARN]`. Pinning is a deliberate, separate, human-approved step.
